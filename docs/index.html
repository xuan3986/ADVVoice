Abstract

Recent neural codec language models have made great progress in the field of Text-to-Speech (TTS), but controllable emotional TTS still faces many challenges. Traditional methods rely on predefined discrete emotion labels to control emotion categories and intensities, which can't capture the complexity and continuity of human emotional expression. Emotional imbalance and limited fine-grained annotations in large-scale emotional text-speech datasets often cause overfitting and impede linear emotion control. To address these issues, we propose UDDETTS, a unified framework for controllable emotional TTS, with a neural codec language model as its core component. UDDETTS introduces the interpretable Arousal-Dominance-Valence (ADV) space and integrates emotion labels for emotion control. It uses semi-supervised training to adapt different dataset types and applies nonlinear binning to mitigate imbalances in ADV space, thereby improving controllable coverage rate of ADV space. Experiments show that UDDETTS achieves linear emotion control along the three dimensions of ADV space, and exhibits superior text-adaptive emotional synthesis capabilities.
